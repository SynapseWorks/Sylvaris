# Story And Intro

## Narrative Philosophy

The player is not a traditional chosen one.

The player becomes important because:
- systems are failing
- resonance is collapsing
- the inherited amulet is damaged
- circumstance created new possibilities

## The Family Amulet

Aurali amulets are inherited through family lines.

The youngest child inherits the amulet because they have the greatest stake in the future.

The player inherits a damaged amulet corrupted generations earlier.

## The Grandparent

The player is raised by their grandparent after inheritance tradition separates the previous bearer from direct parental responsibility.

The grandparent:
- deeply loves the player
- carries hidden guilt
- hides the truth about the amulet
- believes they caused its corruption

The corruption may connect to:
- forbidden resonance
- ancient conflict
- proto-mirror experimentation
- failed sacrifice

## The Player’s Childhood

The player:
- never successfully bonded
- feels spiritually disconnected
- watches other children receive companions
- fears disappointing the tribe

The village believes the player’s resonance may have failed permanently.

## The Spirit Companion

During the coming-of-age ritual, a mysterious spirit responds to the player unexpectedly.

The spirit:
- forms an impossible bond
- stabilizes the damaged amulet
- enables resonance vision
- becomes permanently tied to the player

## Opening Gameplay Structure

### 1. Daily Village Life
Tutorial through:
- gathering
- climbing
- observation
- resonance practice
- village interaction

### 2. Emotional Isolation
Player experiences:
- failed resonance
- subtle pity from villagers
- hidden tension from grandparent

### 3. The Ritual
Sacred coming-of-age ceremony begins.

The damaged amulet destabilizes.

Creatures panic.

### 4. Outside Interference
Faction conflict or attack escalates the crisis.

### 5. Spirit Arrival
The spirit companion answers the player for the first time.

### 6. First Fusion
Instinctive emotional resonance triggers partial fusion.

### 7. Consequence
The village realizes something impossible has happened.

The grandparent admits:
“The amulet was never supposed to answer again.”

## Narrative Endings

The game ends through world-state outcomes rather than single morality endings.

Examples:
- Mirror Ascendancy
- Resonance Rebirth
- Managed Coexistence
- Wild Reclamation
- Fragmented Survival
- Mythic Age

The “best” endings remain fragile and uncertain.

Nature is never fully solved or controlled.
