# Vertical Slice

## Purpose

The vertical slice exists to prove:
- emotional tone
- traversal feel
- creature interaction
- resonance systems
- world atmosphere

It is not intended to represent the full final game scope.

## Slice Length

Target:
20–40 minutes.

## Vertical Slice Goals

The player should finish the slice thinking:
“I need to see more of this world.”

## Included Systems

### Traversal
- sprinting
- climbing
- swimming
- jumping
- dodging

### Resonance Vision
Environmental discovery mechanic.

### Spirit Companion
Basic emotional interaction and guidance.

### One Creature Species
Initial ecological creature interaction.

### Basic Fusion
Echo State and partial Manifest State only.

### Corruption Event
Localized biome instability.

### Dynamic Event
Example:
mirror extraction convoy entering biome.

### Meaningful Choice
Player may:
- intervene
- ignore
- sabotage
- negotiate
- observe

The world reacts accordingly.

## Region

The slice takes place in:
Luma Vale.

Suggested environments:
- village
- river
- forest
- cliffside
- cave
- corrupted zone
- shrine

## Emotional Arc

### Wonder
Beautiful world and exploration.

### Mystery
Something is wrong with resonance.

### Danger
Corruption and faction interference.

### Connection
Spirit companion and first creature bond.

### Transformation
First fusion event.

### Consequence
Player choice affects local ecosystem.

### Curiosity
Player realizes the world is much larger.

## Excluded Systems

Do NOT include:
- giant open world
- advanced economy
- large creature roster
- full weather simulation
- full faction systems
- mythic resonance
- airship gameplay
- advanced crafting

## Success Criteria

The slice succeeds if:
- movement feels magical
- creatures feel alive
- resonance feels emotional
- fusion feels unique
- atmosphere feels memorable
- the world feels reactive

## Prototype Development Order

1. Third-person movement
2. Traversal feel
3. Resonance vision
4. Spirit companion
5. Creature AI
6. Basic fusion
7. One biome environment
8. Dynamic event prototype
