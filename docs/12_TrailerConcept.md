# Trailer Concept

## Trailer Purpose

The first Sylvaris trailer should:
- establish emotional tone
- communicate world identity
- showcase atmosphere
- introduce central conflict
- create curiosity

It should NOT attempt to explain every system.

## Trailer Tone

- mysterious
- emotional
- beautiful
- mythic
- ecological
- cinematic

## Trailer Structure

### Scene 1 — Silence
Wide environmental shots.
Storms over forests.
Ancient ruins.
Narration discussing the fading pact.

### Scene 2 — The Village
The player in Luma Vale.
Other children bonded with creatures.
Player isolated with damaged amulet.

### Scene 3 — The Grandparent
Soft dialogue:
“Some bonds awaken later…”

### Scene 4 — Mirror Civilization
Airships.
Corporate cities.
Mirror deployments.
Industrial extraction.

### Scene 5 — Ecological Conflict
Wildbound sabotage.
Biome instability.
Creature panic.

### Scene 6 — Spirit Arrival
Silence.
The spirit companion appears.
First resonance response.

### Scene 7 — First Fusion
Partial transformation.
Reality distortion.
Weather eruption.

### Scene 8 — Airship Journey
Player’s personal airship crossing storms.

### Final Scene
Question:
“What future will humanity choose?”

Logo:
SYLVARIS

## Visual Style

Target:
- stylized cinematic realism
- ecological fantasy
- emotional atmosphere

## Music Direction

Music progression:
- quiet ambience
- emotional orchestral growth
- mythic climax
- soft unresolved ending
